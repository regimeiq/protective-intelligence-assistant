# Limitations

This repository is a public portfolio project and fixture-backed prototype. It demonstrates workflow design, data modeling, scoring explainability, and reviewable output structure. It should not be read as an operational intelligence service.

## Data Limits

- Synthetic fixtures are used for insider-risk, supply-chain, and cross-domain convergence examples.
- Public companion artifacts use official public sources only and do not include private telemetry, restricted-platform collection, real HR records, or confidential protectee data.
- Public-source context may be stale, incomplete, or too general for a specific itinerary.
- The project does not validate identities, intentions, travel routes, or real-world threat reporting.

## Analytical Limits

- Scores are prioritization aids, not determinations.
- Reason codes explain why the system linked or scored an item; they do not prove causality.
- Correlation can surface plausible pivots but requires analyst review.
- High confidence in data provenance is not the same as high confidence in threat relevance.
- Fixture evaluation metrics are calibration signals for the demo dataset, not field performance claims.

## Operational Limits

- The repository does not provide emergency response guidance.
- It does not replace law enforcement, corporate security, legal, privacy, or threat-assessment review.
- It does not include production-grade access control, data retention governance, or deployment hardening beyond the included safeguards.
- Restricted collectors are environment-gated and should remain disabled unless the operator has appropriate authority, policy coverage, and legal review.

## Public Framing

Use this project as an example of analyst workflow engineering:

- requirements-driven collection
- source and entity normalization
- explainable correlation
- review queues
- casepack generation
- reproducible fixture evaluation

Do not present it as a live protective detail tool, a production UEBA/TPRM platform, or evidence of real investigations.
